This suite of resources helps staff, students and their families to engage in the school exclusion process.

Resources support schools and Education Regional Office staff to understand their roles and responsibilities. Access information guides and downloadable templates.

Supporting resources

Legislation:

  • Work Health and Safety Act 2020 (WA)
    The Act provides for a balanced and nationally consistent framework to secure the health and safety of workers and workplaces.
  • Work Health and Safety (General) Regulations 2022 (WA)
  • School Education Act 1999 (WA)
    This Act makes provision for education in school or by home education with education, training and employment alternatives at the senior secondary level; for the establishment and operation of government schools and for parent and community involvement in school.
  • School Education Regulations 2000 (WA) The Regulations deal with enrolment and attendance, management of government schools.
  • Disability Discrimination Act
    The Act protects the rights of people with disabilities in areas such as housing, education and provision of goods and services.
  • Disability Standards for Education Act
    The Standards clarify the rights of students with disability to access and participate in education and training, and give education providers more guidance on how they can meet their obligations under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992.

Supporting Resources

Policies:

  • Work Health and Safety policy and procedures
    The Department of Education (the Department) will, as far as reasonably practicable, provide and maintain a safe and healthy working and learning environment for all workers and students in its schools and workplaces.
  • Student Behaviour in Public Schools Policy and Procedures
    This policy outlines the mandatory framework for school decision making about promoting and maintaining positive student behaviour, as well as responding to student misconduct.
  • Aboriginal Cultural Standards Framework
    Access the Aboriginal Cultural Standards Framework, support and professional learning to build culturally responsive schools.
  • Incident Management on Department of Education Sites policy
    The Department of Education manages incidents to reduce the likelihood of occurrence; minimise the impact on students, staff and site activities; and facilitate the return of the site to normal operations as soon as possible.

Supporting Resources

Engagement and positive behaviour resources:

  • Understand alternative learning settings
    The alternative learning settings program supports students with violent or aggressive behaviour to develop positive behaviour change and transition back to school or, into a long-term education program.
  • Respecting local culture, history and language
    In this video a principal explain how he recognises and values the cultural traditions of the students and families in a remote community.
  • Alternative attendance arrangement
    Principals can organise a section 24 alternative attendance arrangement for students to temporarily attend another school or off-site educational program.
  • Explicit instruction
    This video explores ways of developing a positive learning environment and high expectations for success with students in a composite class through explicit instruction.
  • Individualising learning
    In this video a teacher uses a range of ICT with her students to individualise student learning to create a culture of learning that fosters an interest in and engagement with languages.
  • Literacy teaching strategies
    In this video a teacher leads a meeting to plan explicit literacy teaching strategies that meet the needs of students from diverse language backgrounds.
  • Supporting children's development
    In this video a teacher engages in self-reflection and then uses effective questioning strategies that address the learning strengths and needs of her children.
  • Differentiation in English
    In this video an English teacher and learning area coordinator work with colleagues to access specialist knowledge, and relevant policy and legislation to ensure teaching programs will support students with disability to participate and learn.

    Access information to create a holistic approach to cyber safety through improving cyber safety and reducing cyber bullying.

    Access lesson plans with practical exercises to reinforce respectful and responsible online behaviours targeting critical thinking and empathy in Years 9 and 10.

    Access resources, professional learning and whole-school approaches to resilience, alcohol and other drug and road safety education from birth to 18 years of age.

    Access professional learning that teaches staff how to recognise the signs that should raise concerns of child abuse, including information about possible grooming behaviours, how to respond to suspected child abuse and how to teach protective behaviours.
