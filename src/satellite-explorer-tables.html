Satellite: Hubble Space Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST) is a space telescope that was carried into orbit by a space shuttle in April 1990. The telescope is expected to function until at least 2014, when its successor, the James Web Telescope is due to be launched.

Satellite: Iridium 98

Iridium 98 is one of a large group of satellites used to provide voice and data coverage to satellite phones, pagers and integrated tranceivers over Earth's entire surface. 66 Iridium satellites are in current use, with several spares also in orbit.

Satellite: GPS

Global Positioning System (GPS) was developed by the US Department of Defense to provide all-weather, round-the-clock navigation for military ground, sea and air forces. It is now used in many civilian applications and industries around the globe. GPS employs 24 spacecraft in circular orbits inclined at 53.6 degrees.
