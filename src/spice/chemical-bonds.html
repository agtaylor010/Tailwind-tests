Chemical bonds

What are chemical bonds?

Chemical bonds join atoms together to create larger structures sich as molecules and cystals. The nature of a chemical bond (or even the absence of bonds) can explain the chemical and physical properties of substances.

The following page looks are three different types of chemical bonds; ionic, metallic and covalent.

Chemical bonds hold the atoms of hydrogen and oxygen in a molecule of water.

Ionic bonds

Ionic bonds form between a metal (such as lithium, sodum or calcium) and a non-metal (such as oxygen or chlorine).

Properties of ionic compounds

Can you explain the properties of a typical ionic compound by looking at these different structures?

Typical ionic compounds, such as sodium chloride:

  • form brittle cystals,
  • conduct electricity when molten or in aqueous solution, but nor when solid,
  • have high melting points, high boiling points and,
  • are generally soluble in water.

  • have low melting and boiling points,
  • have low solubility in water, and

Covalent networks

If an atom can form three or moe covalent bonds with neighbouring atoms it is possible to create a giant, repeating structure called a covalent network.

Can you explain the properties of a typical covalent network solid by looking at these different structures?

Typical covalent network solids:

  • are very hard,
  • do not conduct electricity, and
  • have high melting and boiling points.
