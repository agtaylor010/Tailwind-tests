Excretory system

Kidney

(Low - 250µm)

This view shows part of the renal cortex, the outer region of the kidney that is involved in filtration and absorption. Several renal corpuscles are labelled. A renal corpuscle is where urine formation starts and it is composed of the Bowman's capsule and glomerulus. The medullary ray is an extension of tissue from the medulla, or inner region of the kidney. The ray is a group of collecting tubules.

(Medium - 100µm)

In addition to the glomeruli, you should be able to see several proximal and distal convoluted tubules. The pink blur within the proximal convoluted tubules is caused by microvilli that cannot be seen clearly at this magnification. The distal tubules seem to be clear and their walls are thinner than the proximal tubules. Clear, thicker-walled tubules are collecting ducts.

(High - 50µm)

The outer layer of this image shows a Bowman's capsule that encases a glomerulus. Proximal tubules, distal tubules and capillaries are visible. The large nuclei in the glomerulus belong to podocytes, cells that wrap around glomerular capillaries to create a filtration barrier. Mesangial cells are modified smooth muscle cells that tug on the capillaries and control blood flow through the glomerulus.

Ureter

(Low - 250µm)

The cytoplasm of these cells is lightly stained purple while the nuclei are small black dots.

(Medium - 50µm)

At medium magnification, the cells are easier to see. They are oval in shape and the nuclei tend to be centrally located in each cell.

(High - 50µm)

The plasma membrane surrounds the cell. It controls the movement of substances into and out of the cell. Another membrane encircles the nucleus.

Bladder

(Low - 250µm)

The skin has two layers (the epidermis and the dermis). Below the dermis lies the subcutaneous region. Different layers can be seen within the epidermis, but they are clearer at higher magnifications. The dermis is composed of dense fibro-elastic material and is where hair follicles and sweat glands are located.

(Medium - 50µm)

Five layers can be seen in the epidermis. The large number of individual cells visible in the stratum basale indicates a high rate of cell division. Cells are constantly needed to replace the dead cells of the outer layer, the stratum corneum. The stratum corneum is between 15 and 20 cell layers thick.

(High - 50µm)

Cells of the stratum basale are quite distinct in this image. They are columnar or cuboidal in shape while cells in the more outer layers are flattened. Mitosis (cell division) does not occur in the outer three layers.
